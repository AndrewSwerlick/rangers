# The Rangers — Art & Typography Guide

## Visual Direction

The game's art direction draws from two complementary historical traditions:

- **WPA Posters (1930s–40s):** Monumental, graphic, condensed letterforms. Bold geometry. A sense of institutional reverence for the natural world.
- **Naturalist Field Guides (19th–early 20th century):** Precise, scholarly, old-style serif typography. The visual language of careful scientific observation.

These two registers pull in different tonal directions but share a common subject — the natural world — which makes them coherent as a combined identity. The three-layer typographic system below maps onto the three emotional registers of the game itself: monumental, scholarly, and personal.

---

## Typographic System

### Layer 1: Headers & Section Titles — WPA Register

Used for: major headers, park names, act titles, wave names.

**Primary recommendation:** Josefin Sans Bold
- Art Deco refinement; reads like a Department of the Interior document
- More restrained than League Gothic; better for a rules document

**Alternative:** League Gothic
- Stronger condensed poster energy; more authentically 1930s
- Better for pure display use (covers, large callouts)

**Avoid:** Bebas Neue — overused, has lost period authenticity.

---

### Layer 2: Body Text — Field Guide Register

Used for: rules text, stat descriptions, location abilities, all running prose.

**Primary recommendation:** EB Garamond
- Genuine scholarly weight; reads well at 10–11pt
- Excellent italics for flavor text and annotations
- Feels like a Peterson Field Guide, not a generic RPG product

**Alternative:** Cardo
- Also designed for humanist/classical texts
- Slightly more open than Garamond at small sizes

---

### Layer 3: Accent Text — Field Notes Register

Used for: handwritten ranger annotations, margin notes, flavor text framed as personal field notes, character sheet personal details.

**Handwritten feel:** Caveat
- Clean enough to remain readable; feels genuinely handwritten
- Good for personal ranger details and in-world annotations

**Typewriter feel:** Special Elite
- Works well for anything framed as official correspondence, forms, or reports

---

## Recommended Working Palette

| Role | Font | Use |
|------|------|-----|
| Major headers | Josefin Sans Bold | Act titles, section headers, park name |
| Body text | EB Garamond | All rules text, descriptions |
| Flavor/quotes | EB Garamond Italic | Field note excerpts, thematic quotes |
| Annotations | Caveat | Handwritten margin notes, personal character details |

---

## Illustration Style

Illustrations should reference real park photography as source material, maintaining thematic consistency with the map-based mechanics. The naturalist field guide aesthetic favors:

- Precise linework over painterly rendering
- Subject isolation (species, landmarks) against clean or minimal backgrounds
- Annotated diagrams where appropriate
- Muted, naturalistic color palettes consistent with WPA poster conventions (limited palette, flat color, strong silhouette)

---

## Practical Notes

- All recommended fonts are available on **Google Fonts** and free for print use.
- For the LaTeX rulebook: EB Garamond has a solid LaTeX package (`ebgaramond`); Josefin Sans and Caveat can be brought in via XeLaTeX as system fonts.
- For character sheets and reference materials in Affinity Publisher or similar: all fonts are directly available via Google Fonts.
- League Gothic and Josefin Sans have wide spacing assumptions — test at target sizes before committing to layout.
